Irrigation Project

This year we are collaborating with students from the Engineering department at Warwick University to solve one of the largest practical challenges the site faces every year… Irrigation!

Project specifications are as below.

Fenced Area - Width: 20.4M. Length: 13.6M.

Polytunnel - Width: 4.4M. Length: 10.2M.

Water Requirements: At an average of 17L per raised bed with around 22.5 raised beds in total, the volume of water required to cover the entire area would be around 380L. This is for the biggest use case scenario, on a very hot day that would dry up the plants. Regular usage will be lower and as such may not constitute the need for a water tank which holds maximum capacity.

The polytunnel will only require a maximum of two loads of 50L of water with the same extreme environments on a hot day. Majority of the time the load case will be around half of the amount stated.

Our Requirements: Must be an off-grid system with the aim of being sustainable to stick true to the society’s culture. It’s important to try and emulate the values of sustainability and self-sufficiency, something which is so important in a world where national infrastructure sometimes falls short and where we want to remind people of their personal power as a collective to problem-solve and get things done!

This project is just as much political and educational as it is practical.
